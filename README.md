# Course Objective and Plan
from syllabus Houston City College Spring 2026

## Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare
There is no richer area for A.I. applications that in healthcare. Generative AI
has the potential to transform how humans learn, work and interact with one another
in every aspect of life providing great benefits to patients adn healthcare providers.
Healthcare has the greatest need of any business sector to take advantage of A.I.
capabilities from chatbots to internal training, patient services, compliance, automated 
processes and crucial information. Many large hospitals now have A.I. Innovation Centers
to solve problems and help grow novel solutions and relationships with A.I. developers.

## What we plan to achieve in this course:
- Build foundational skills in AI project planning and emerging technologies like LLMs and agents.
- Explore AI applications and domain expertise across healthcare subsectors: pharmaceuticals, clinical care, diagnostics, imaging, and administration.
- Demonstrate integrated knowledge through hands-on project developments and presentations **<br>
** note: some projects include - Contact Tracing, Testing and Monitoring & Automated Smart Call Center 
application for large numbers of emergency distress calls.

## Some of the Learning Objectives for this Course:
- **Foundational Knowledge**: Cover AI fundamentals, machine learning algorithms, and healthcare
domain essentials, ensuring students grasp core concepts and terminology relevant to healthcare AI.
- **Interview Questions**: Integrate interview-focused sessions including common technical and behavioral 
questions, mock interviews, and communication skill workshops tailored to AI in healthcare roles.
- **Hands-on Projects**: Incorporate practical projects each module that involve implementing AI 
models on healthcare datasets, building prototypes (e.g., medical image classification, NLP on clinical notes).
- **AI Applications Focus**: Deep dives into AI uses in diagnostics, therapeutics, clinical decision 
support, public health, and healthcare operations to build domain-specific expertise.
